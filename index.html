Friends

About the Show

Friends is an American sitcom that aired on NBC from September 22, 1994, to May 6, 2004.

The show is set in Manahattan and revolves around a group of six friends in their 20s and 30s as they navigate through the ups and downs of life, love, career, and friendship.

It is known for its humor, beloved characters, and memorable catchphrases.

The show falls under the genre of sitcom (situation comedy), a comedy following the same set of characters facing various situations every episode.

Main Characters

  • Rachel Green - played by Jennifer Aniston
  • Monica Geller - played by Courteney Cox
  • Phoebe Buffay - played by Lisa Kudrow
  • Joey Tribbiani - played by Matt LeBlanc

About the Characters

Friends follows six friends in their 20s and 30s:

  • Rachel Green: The fashionable, charismatic barista
  • Monica Geller: The energetic, neat-freak chef
  • Phoebe Buffay: The eccentric and free-spirited masseuse
  • Joey Tribbiani: The actor with a love of food
  • Chandler Bing: The sarcastic, comedic office worker
  • Ross Geller: The goofy, lovable paleontologist

Some prominent side characters include:

  • Janice Litman-Goralnik: Chandler's on-again, off-again girlfriend
  • Gunther: The manager of Central Perk
  • Richard Burke: An older love interest of Monica's and a family friend of the Geller's
  • Mike Hannigan: Phoebe's love interest and eventual husband
  • Emily Waltham: Ross's second wife from England

These characters, along with many others, contribute to the foundational relationships and stories in Friends.
